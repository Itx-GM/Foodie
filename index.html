What is a Food Web?

A food web is a complex network of interconnected food chains that show how energy and nutrients flow through an ecosystem. It represents the feeding relationships between organisms such as plants, herbivores, carnivores, and decomposers.

Main Components

  • Producers: Plants that make food through photosynthesis.
  • Consumers: Animals that eat plants or other animals.
  • Decomposers: Organisms like fungi and bacteria that break down dead matter.
